The Guide to Wireframing

What are the advantages and disadvantages of sketching wireframes over wireframe software?

Advantages:

Sketching wireframes on paper or on a hard copy is quick and easy, convenient. You can do it anywhere and without any technology. Another advantage is that you can convey your thoughts better without the boundaries of wireframing software. Instead of compromising your design with a template that they provide, you could draw out your true intentions. While sketching on paper, it is also easier to focus on the structure of the wireframe versus the design. With software, adding designs can easily distract from the main task of structure, making it simple and easy in writing.

Disadvantages:

When it comes to the disadvantages of sketching out wireframes, there are a lot. Sketching is very messy and is hard to edit, not to mention it can also be drawn ugly to where it can be illegible. It can also be hard to have consistency throughout a sketch, and it would be hard to show the intentions of different fonts, animations, certain pictures, etc. One of the main points of wireframing is to easily share the idea and structure with others. With a hard copy, it would be difficult to share and collaborate with other people. Not to mention, it would have to be that one copy that is edited and having access to that can be proven difficult.
