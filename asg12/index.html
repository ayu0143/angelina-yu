Favorite Movie Gallery

Majority of my favorite movies are animations, with deep adventurous plots. Some of the animations and live actions are directed by Tim Burton, whom has a famous style and concept in his movies. Some others are just old animated fantasy-based Disney movies, and others are just comedies. When it comes to my favorite movies, I have to be able to rewatch them time to time without getting sick of them. One day, I'll just suddenly feel the need to watch one of the movies listed below and I never find myself dissapointed.

Alice in Wonderland

One of my favorite renditions of Lewis Carroll's Alice in Wonderland is Tim Burton's live action starring Mia Wasikowska as Alive, Johnny Depp as the Mad Hatter, Anne Hathaway as the White Queen, and Helena Bonham Carter as the Red Queen. If unfamiliar with Alice in Wonderland, it's about a girl who falls down a rabbit hole and into a world called Wonderland. She fights to save Wonderland from the evil Red Queen with the help of her friends.

Anastasia

Anastasia is an animated musical, based on the speculations of the lost Romanov Anastasia. It features an orphan girl who lost her memories and throughout the movie regains them to finally remember who she is and where she's from. There's romance, comedy, and adventure that makes it so enjoyable.

The Lost City of Atlantis

This animated movie is about an adventure about finding the Lost City of Atlantis. There's betrayal, romance, and friendship throughout this movie. It gives it drama and excitment with the search for the lost city, and it never gets boring.

Coraline

Coraline is a stop-motion animated film directed by Henry Selick. It is made for children, but is well known for even creeping out adults. It's based off of Neil Gaiman's fantasy horror written for children. Selick does a great job creating personalities to characters, and the wonderful thrill to see Coraline defeat her enemies.

Corpse Bride

This film is a stop-motion animated film directed by Tim Burton. It follows the main character Victor Van Dort who finds himself in an arranged marriage. While practicing his vows in the forest, he accidentally finds himself eloping with a corpse bride. The premise of the movie is finding a way to solve Victor's obstacles in an thrilling ride.

Cruella

Cruella is a reimagined live action of 101 Dalmations, featuring the backstory of the main villian Cruella DeVille. The main premise of this movie is Cruella's double personality, and her climb to the top with fashion and trickery. It is a well-made live action that is fun and attention-drawing, espcially if you have an eye for fashion.

James and the Giant Peach

This film is a mixture of live action and stop-motion animation, also animated by Tim Burton. The themes are less dark than his other films, but still find the viewer at the edge of their seat at times. It's an interesting concept where this orphan boy names James finds himself surrounded by life-sized bugs that help him get to the New York Empire State Building via a flying peach.
